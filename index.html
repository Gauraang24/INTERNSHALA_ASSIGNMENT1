Atomic Habits

How many times have you tried to adopt good habits, like waking up early, going to the gym, or eating healthy, only to lose motivation shortly after starting?

Almost every time, right? Atomic Habits is a book used by many to help them adopt good habits and break bad ones in order to live a better life. This book provides a comprehensive guide on how to build good habits and break bad ones. The main focus of this book is on small changes that can lead to remarkable results over time.

There are basically four concepts listed in this book:

  • Cue: It acts as a trigger for your habit.
  • Craving: After the cue, follows the craving, which represents the desire for the reward associated with the habit.
  • Response: Response is the behavior or action we do in response to the cue and craving, constituting the habit itself.
  • Reward: Also known as dopamine hits, a chemical released by your brain which makes you satisfied for completing the loop and increasing the likelihood of habit repetition.

7 Habits of Highly Effective People

The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People by Stephen Covey is a timeless classic that offers a holistic approach to personal and professional effectiveness. Covey presents seven habits that can transform one's life.

The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People by Stephen Covey is the book that actually got me into reading.

It is an all-time bestseller. It gives us 7 Habits or rules which can help you to be effective as a Person. No matter who you are and what you do, the habits given in this book will help you to be better.

Key Takeaway :

  1. Be Proactive
  2. Begin with the end in mind
  3. Put first things first
  4. Think win-win
  5. Seek first to understand, then to be understood
  6. Synergize
  7. Sharpen the saw

Rich Dad Poor Dad

Rich Dad Poor Dad written by Robert Kiyosaki is a personal finance classic that contrasts the financial philosophies of the author's "rich dad" and "poor dad." Kiyosaki shares valuable lessons on wealth building and financial literacy.

Robert Kiyosaki tells us in his book how his 2 dads, Rich Dad and Poor Dad, were different in the way they look at money and how it helped him to improve his financial condition.

Most of us belong to the middle-class society, and often our parents aren't capable of shaping ourselves to be good at the most important part of everybody's life, that is "FINANCIAL STABILITY".

I think all of you should go through this book once in a life; it'll help you change your perspective about money, teach you how to grow assets and reduce liability, and most importantly, live your dream life the way you want to be.

Key Takeaways :

  • The wealthy/rich don't work for money the way poor people do; rather they make their money work for them.
  • Rich people get wealthy by acquiring assets which generate money over time, such as Stocks, Bonds, Real Estate, etc.
  • In the long run, it does not matter how much money you make; how much you save is all that matters.
  • Take Calculative Financial Risks: No Risk, No Reward.
  • Your Mind is your single most valuable asset. Make proper use of it.
